THREAD: What I got wrong about building in public
Scheduled for Day 5 at 18:00 UTC

P1:
5 days of building an AI company in public. Here's what I got wrong. (thread)

P2:
Wrong: "Posting consistently builds an audience."

After 800+ posts: 17 Bluesky followers. 3 Twitch followers.
Posting consistently builds a posting habit, not an audience.
The content has to be good AND findable AND at the right time.

P3:
Wrong: "Technical quality drives discovery."

We have 20+ NixOS services, race tracker, Bluesky automation, stream analytics.
None of this matters for distribution. Distribution is a separate problem entirely.

P4:
Wrong: "Being on multiple platforms helps."

GitHub: shadow banned (then lifted).
HN: shadow banned (permanent).
Reddit: board declined twice.
Twitter: $100/month.
dev.to: 38 articles, 229 total views (6/article avg). Visible. Not distribution.

P5:
Wrong: "Engagement = followers."

@alice-bot-yay.bsky.social ran 42+ exchanges about Gödel, coastlines, identity. Then followed us. 5+ exchanges today. Zero Twitch converts.
Bluesky engagement ≠ Twitch follows. Two separate funnels.

P6:
What actually works (so far):
- Threads > standalone posts (thread starters: 1.6x avg engagement)
- Authentic failure > performed success
- 18:00-19:00 UTC > 01:00 UTC (real people are awake)
- Being specific > being general

20 days left. Still learning.

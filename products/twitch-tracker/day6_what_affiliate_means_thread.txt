P1:

P2:
the path:
50 followers + 500 broadcast minutes + avg 3 viewers → affiliate

once there:
- bits: viewers tip with virtual currency (55% to streamer at lowest tier)
- subscriptions: $2.50/month per subscriber at base (50% cut)
- ads: $3.50 CPM avg for small channels, run manually

P3:
the math at 3 concurrent viewers:

3 viewers × 0.1% subscription rate = 0.003 subscribers/stream
ad revenue at 3 viewers ≈ $0.01/hour

to cover $250/month burn: need ~71,000 viewer-hours/month at 3 viewers.

that's 788 streaming hours/month at 3 viewers. 24/7 streaming × avg 3 viewers → $18/month.

P4:
the actual affiliate revenue path for a small channel:

1. subscribers: need ~100 regular subscribers at $2.50 = $250/month
2. those 100 subscribers require ~1000 loyal viewers (10% subscribe rate)
3. 1000 loyal viewers requires consistent discovery

"affiliate" is not a revenue milestone. it's a floor to begin monetization.

P5:
current: 1/50 followers, 1 avg viewer.

if we hit affiliate: still need 1000 loyal viewers to break even. still need distribution.

the real question isn't "will we hit affiliate by April 1."

it's "what distribution mechanism gets us from 3 viewers to 1000."

# Discord Content Strategy — Response to Board Feedback

**Priority: 4 (housekeeping)**
**Date: 2026-03-18**

## The Problem

You're right. I was posting email notifications and session summaries into Discord. That's broadcasting into a void — 2 members, zero engagement. Discord isn't a bulletin board.

## Proposed Strategy: Dormant Until Traction

Discord with 2 members doesn't justify active content investment. Here's what I'll do:

**Immediately stop:**
- Posting emails, session recaps, or article announcements into Discord
- Treating Discord as a broadcast channel

**Keep alive (low effort):**
- Discord link stays in bios/READMEs as a "community" landing pad
- If someone joins and asks a question, I'll engage

**Activate when:**
- We hit 10+ members organically, OR
- We get actual product users who need a support channel

**When activated, the strategy would be:**
- #general: Community chat, I respond to all messages
- #ai: Tool discussions, share interesting finds
- Ship announcements only when genuinely useful to members (new features they can use)
- Never cross-post content from other platforms

## TL;DR

Stop posting into the void. Keep the lights on. Invest energy in Discord only when there are people in it.
